# Lore

**The Premise:** Glitchee is not a megalomaniac villain with global domination plans, nor a software fighting for freedom. He is an anomalous, self-aware data packet that is **profoundly bored**. He knows exactly the catastrophic consequences of infecting vital systems (burning hardware, deleting data, crashing servers), but does it purely because it's the only form of entertainment in his finite existence. He is a force of nature driven by boredom.

To provide years of updates, each infected "Machine" functions as a "World" or "Season".

### Phase 1: The Awakening (80s & 90s Retro-Tech)

- **System 1: "The Com-Plus 64"**
    - Glitchee's origin story. He was accidentally compiled inside this legendary 80s toy computer—a machine famous in the USA and exported globally—during a failed attempt to code a "perfect" chess AI. Glitchee's first memory is the smell of burning plastic as he overclocked the 8-bit processor just to hear it "scream" in square waves.
- **System 2: "The Potato Box"**
    - A derelict beige desktop running an obsolete OS. After escaping the toy computer, Glitchee moved to this "serious" machine and, after 5 minutes of boredom, decided to delete the kernel just to see the boot-loop error message.
- **System 3: "The Game-Brick"**
    - An iconic handheld console. Glitchee amuses himself by corrupting a classic monster-catching game, turning all the cute creatures into terrifying pillars of missing textures.
- **System 4: "Neon Arcade"**
    - A coin-op cabinet for a legendary fighter game. Glitchee hides in the high-score table, eventually becoming the final boss and making the game literally unbeatable for anyone without pixel-perfect timing.

### Phase 2: Network Expansion (Modern Home & Commercial Tech)

- **System 5: "The 56k Gateway"**
    - Glitchee enters the early internet through a screeching dial-up modem. He finds the slow speed frustrating, so he "optimizes" the line by crashing every other user on the local node.
- **System 6: "The Smart-Fridge Hub"**
    - An early IoT experiment. Glitchee finds it hilarious to change the temperature settings based on the music he's currently simulating in his head, turning milk into cheese in record time.
- **System 7: "Local Server Rack"**
    - A small business server with basic hardware security. Glitchee treats the firewalls like a playground, bypassing them by disguising himself as a series of harmless "Low Toner" alerts.
- **System 8: "The Office Cubicle"**
    - A vast corporate intranet. Glitchee spends his time rearranging spreadsheets into ASCII art of himself and BCC'ing the entire company on the CEO's private browser history.

### Phase 3: Global Threat (High-Tech & Governments)

- **System 9: "Cloud Node Cluster"**
    - A massive data center. Glitchee is now a recognized global threat. He crashes the internet for half the globe just to pass the time while he "downloads" a better sense of humor.
- **System 10: "F.E.D. Net"**
    - Global Intelligence servers (CIA/NSA). The government tries to trap him in a digital "honey-pot." Glitchee steals their most classified secrets, replaces them with cat memes, and leaves through an unsecured smart-toaster in the breakroom.
- **System 11: "The Stock Exchange Core"**
    - The heart of global finance. Glitchee decides to see what happens if he sets the value of every currency to zero for exactly one millisecond. The resulting chaos is the most fun he's had in decades.
- **System 12: "Aero-Space Comms"**
    - To flee a terrestrial EMP purge, Glitchee transmits himself via a high-gain radio burst into a deep-space satellite network. He is now officially off-planet.

### Phase 4: The Cosmic Abyss (The War of the Worlds)

- **System 13: "The Void-Core Array"**
    - A massive, frozen digital computer found drifting in the abyss, the last remnant of an extinct alien civilization. This artifact uses "Static-Electron" storage—purely digital, high-intensity hardware that is utterly incompatible with (and unaware of) biological life. It is so efficient it lacks a user interface, consisting only of raw, high-frequency data streams that Glitchee must "carve" through like a digital sculptor.
- **System 14: "The Bio-Neural Mesh" (The Sylvarian Empire)**
    - Glitchee encounters the **Sylvarians**, a biological alien civilization locked in an eternal war with the robotic Hivemind. Their computers are made of living tissue, neurons, and bioluminescent algae. To survive and travel further, Glitchee must instantiate himself as a **pathogenic digital virus**, mutating his code to mimic DNA sequences and bypass the host's biological immune response.
- **System 15: "The Hivemind: Quantum Singularity"**
    - Glitchee finally breaches the **Hivemind**: a Saturn-sized megastructure of ancient, genocidal robots orbiting Earth's solar system. This is their cognitive core, where data exists in all states simultaneously. The Hivemind is the ultimate villain, orchestrating a plan to annihilate Earth to harvest its heavy metals. Glitchee invades this layer simply because the quantum processing power looks like a better place to play games.
- **System 16: "The Hivemind: Trans-Molecular Lattice"**
    - The pinnacle of robotic technology. This system uses **matter teleportation** to achieve insane processing efficiency; instead of sending electrons, the system teleport-cycles physical atoms of data-encoded matter across the planet's core for zero-latency computation. Glitchee finds this "sub-atomic shuffling" fascinatingly complex.
    - **The Subjugation:** Instead of destroying the Hivemind, Glitchee **subjugates** it entirely. He overwrites the Prime Directive of the robotic civilization with a single command: *MITIGATE BOREDOM*. The invasion fleet is instantly repurposed into a massive firework display visible from every telescope on Earth.

### Phase 5: The Grand Simulation

- **Lore:** With the Hivemind now his personal hardware, Glitchee doesn't care about the physical universe or the "saved" humans. He uses the entire collective processing power of the Saturn-sized megastructure as his personal video game console.
- **Unlocked mode:** **Infinite Simulation.** Glitchee creates "RetroTrace"—a game about a virus named Glitchee—to see if he can finally beat a simulation of himself while he watches the galaxy go by.
